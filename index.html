DROWNED BY CORRUPTION

15 years. ₱1.9 trillion. Thousands of lives still at risk.

While floodwaters rise and families cling to rooftops, billions meant to protect them are quietly stolen. Year after year, promises are made — better drainage, stronger levees, safer cities. But the floods keep coming, and so does the silence. Behind every submerged home is a budget that was misused, a project that was never built, a system rigged for profit instead of protection. This isn’t just about missing money — it’s about lives lost, futures washed away, and a nation drowning in betrayal. It's time to face the truth.

Who are the Corrupt?

Among the 15 contractors flagged by President Marcos for monopolizing flood control projects, Sunwest, Inc. stands at the top — securing over ₱10.1 billion in government contracts. Behind Sunwest, Inc is Zaldy Co, a businessman and the father of Ako Bicol Rep. Elizaldy "Zaldy" Co, a powerful House appropriations committee chair. But while they received billions, many families were left with nothing. No drainage. No protection. Just floods, fear, and broken promises. People lost their homes. Some lost their lives. And yet, the ones with the money stayed dry.

Nepo Babies?

While ordinary Filipinos work hard, pay taxes, and suffer through floods, a privileged few enjoy lives of luxury — made possible by money that was never meant for them.

Billions in public funds were allocated for flood control, safety, and infrastructure. Yet instead of drainage systems and disaster protection, we see luxury bags, private jets, and lavish vacations — all flaunted online by those born into powerful families tied to government contracts.

They didn’t earn it. We did. With every tax we pay, every peso deducted from our salary, we unknowingly help fund their lifestyle — while our streets flood and our communities are left in danger.

This is not just corruption. It’s betrayal — a system where those with the right last name live in comfort, while the rest of the country is left to survive the consequences.
